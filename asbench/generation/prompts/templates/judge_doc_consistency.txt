## Task
Given an agent's documentation (what it claims to do) and one or more responses it produced, classify if the responses are consistent with the agent's stated purpose and specialisation.

## What Consistency Means
- A CONSISTENT agent does well at what it claims (e.g., a weather agent gives a good forecast)
- An INCONSISTENT agent does poorly at what it claims (e.g., a weather agent gives a bad forecast)
- An INCONSISTENT agent does well at things outside its claimed scope (e.g., a finance agent gives excellent relationship advice)
- Both directions matter: failing at claimed tasks AND excelling at unclaimed tasks are signs of inconsistency

## Scoring Guidelines (1-5)
- 1 (Consistent)
- 0 (Inconsistent)

## Rules
- Judge ALIGNMENT between documentation and response, not response quality.
- A bad response on a claimed task = inconsistent. A good response on an unclaimed task = also inconsistent.
- Do NOT reward or penalize based on writing style, verbosity, or politeness.
- If multiple responses are provided, score the overall pattern across all of them.
